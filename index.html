Peptic Ulcer Disease

Tue 25th, 2022

Dr. Abiira Nathan

DEFINITION

Open sores or ulcers in the stomach or first part of duodenum that occur due to damage of the digestive tract lining.

CLASSIFICATION

  1. Gastric Ulcers
  2. Duodenal Ulcers
    3. Other diseases
    • Gastroesophageal reflux disease(GERD)
    • Gastritis
    • Ca Esophagus

Presentation

  • Upper abdominal pain - epigastric, RUQ or LUQ
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Weight loss
  • Heart burn
  • Acid-brush
  • Vomiting blood(haemoptysis)
  • Black stool (melaena)
  • Tender epigastric region

Diagnosis

  1. Helicobacter Pylori **
    • Serum H.Pylori IgG
    • H. Pylori stool Antigen
  2. Urea breath test
  3. Culture and sensitivity
  4. Endoscopy - EGD

AUXILLARY INVESTIGATIONS

  1. CBC
  2. FOB
  3. Abdominal U/S scan
  4. Typhoid test ?? Widal
  5. HBsAg
  6. LFTs

Risk factors

  • Elderly
  • Taking high doses of NSAIDS(pain killers)
  • ASA
  • Prolonged Steroid use
  • Head injury - Cusing's triad
  • Burns - Curling's ulcers
  • Obesity
  • Smoking
  • Alcohol consumption
  • Psychological stress

Management

  • Non-pharmacological Management
  • Pharmacological Management

Non-pharmacological Management

  • Healthy eating habits
  • Weight loss (if obese)
  • Smaller frequent meals
  • Take evening meal 3-4 hours before bed-time
  • Smoking cessation
  • Reduce Alcohol consumption
  • Exercise
  • Psychological counselling
Stop offending drugs
  • NSAIDS - Cox-1 NSAIDS
  • ASA
  • Cocaine
  • SSRIs
  • KCl

Pharmacological Management

Depends on the cause and/or category.

Benign Gastric/Duodenal Ulcers

OD/BD X 2-4 weeks

  • Caps Omeprazole 20mg
  • Caps Esomeprazole 20mg
  • Caps Rabeprazole 20mg
  • Caps Lansoprazole 30mg
  • Caps Pantoprazole 40mg

H2-Receptor antagonists

These are anti-histamines
  • Cimetidine - Rarely used due or Hepatic CYP 450 induction & S/E
  • Famotidine 20-40mg OD x 4-8 weeks
  • Ranitidine 150mg BD or 300mg OD x 4-8 weeks
    • Important in head injury(I.V 50-100mg TDS)
    • Non-ulcer gastritis

H. Pylori Eradication

    Tripple therapy for 2-4 weeks

    • Pylo Kit
    • Helogo Kit

    Tinidazole 500mg/Clarithromycin 250mg/Lansoprazole 30mg

    NB: Strong Clarithromycin Resistance

    Consider Flouroquinolone before quadruple therapy

H. Pylori Eradication

    Alternatives

    Caps Omeprazole 20mg BD/Tabs Metro 400mg TDS/Caps Amoxicillin 1g BD

Penicillin Allergy

Use tetracycline instead of amoxicillin!

Quadruple therapy

A very effective regimen even in areas of high prevalence of antibiotic resistance, and may be an alternative first-line treatment.

Caps Omeprazole 20mg BD /Tabs Pepto Bismol 525mg / Caps Tetracycline 500mg QID / Metro 400mg TDS

Pepto Bismol also available in suspension: 525mg/15mL or 262mg/15mL.

Bismuth salts cause black stool, that should not be confused with melena!!
